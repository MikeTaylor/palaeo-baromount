# Notes on boxes from Dinosaur National Monument


## Introduction

This document contains information relavant to the AMNH _Barosaurus_ that was extracted from `DNM record book 1909-1923.doc`. The relevant field numbers are:
* #340 (the _Barosaurus_ that would later be accessioned at the Americal Museum of Natural History as AMNH 6341)
* #355 (a _Diplodocus_ that would later be accessioned at the United States National Museum as USNM 10865).

Relevant box numbers include 348/A and 348/B (both mentioned in Brown 1929).


## Not relevant

* Box 340, which contained a skull of _Dolichorinus_ (brontothere), the mandible of a large carnivirous mammal and a titanothere humerus.
* Box 355, which contained a _Camarasaurus_.


## Relevant

* No. 340  SKELETON OF DIPLODOCUS.
  * The larger portion of the neck was taken out by the Carnegie Museum but was turned over to the United States National Museum.
  * Block 340/D was shipped to the Carnegie where the end of the Tail and the Tail Spines of Stegosaurus, No. 350, are to be removed and the portion of the neck of the Diplodocus which is in the block is to be shipped to the U.S. National Museum.  
  * Block 340/E, which contains a Foot of the above named Stegosaurus, was shipped to the U.S. National Museum and the foot is to be returned to the Carnegie Museum.  
  * The U.S.N.M. took all of the spinal column of this specimen to the 5th Dorsal including a fore limb.  Remainder being taken out by University of Utah.  (Afterwards procured by American Museum of Nat. Hist. Kay (Handwritten in after entry) 
  * Box 440

* No. 348  SAUROPOD DINOSAUR.  Apparently Diplodocus.  Barosaurus.  (V: e142).
  * Taken out in making tunnel for storage room etc. 
  * Probably part of the tail of No. 340.
  * No. 348/A—Three Caudal Vertebrae.  (V. e141).
  * No. 348/Aa—Caudal Vertebra.   (Va: e143).
  * No. 348/B—Three Caudal Vertebrae of Diplodocus and Humerus of Stegosaurus.    (V: e144).
  * Out Sent to American Museum.

* No. 355  DIPLODOCUS.
  * Nearly complete skeleton except head and neck.
  * General location (V: e128).
  * Note—Not Taken out for the Carnegie Museum.  Still in rock Jan. 20, 1923.
  * Taken out by U.S. National Museum in Summer of 1923.

* BOX 404. All out. 2 blocks in Box very poor, discarded March 1-1960
  * No. 347--Caudal Vertebra. -> C.M. 11878 returned from American Museum caudal part of limb bones 
  * No. 348/A--Caudal Vertebrae. Three.
  * Block 348/Aa--Caudals. 
  * Block 348/B--Humerus and Caudal.

* BOX 440. out Opened July 19 1924 by J.L.Kay
  * Block 340/--Cervicals of 340 and Tail Spines of Stegosaurus 350. (These Cervicals to be sent to the U.S. National Museum after tail is removed, as they have balance of neck of this specimen).

* BOX 444. out A small blk, BX# 444 Blk# rubbed off. A carpal bone.

  * Block 350/A--Part of Foot of Stegosaurus. The greater part of the foot in Block 340/E. Part in Bl.345/C. 
  * NOTE--This block (340/E) contains part of the neck of No. 340.
This goes to the U.S. National Museum, and after the block is worked out the Foot is to be sent to the Carnegie Museum where the remainder of this nearly complete skeleton is gone.
